Malai Kofta Recipe

Description

An extremely popular and tasty north indian creamy curry recipe made with aloo and paneer balls in onion and tomato sauce. It is one of the creamiest curry recipe from the North Indian cuisine bucket and loaded with malai or cooking cream. You may either serve this recipe with choice of indian flatbreads or perhaps with choice of flavoured rice

Ingredients

  • For Kofta
    • 3 potato,boiler & mashed
    • ¾ cup paneer,grated
    • 1 chilli, finely chopped
    • 2 tbsp coriander,finely chopped
    • ¼ tsp cumin powder
    • ½ tsp salt
    • 2 tbsp raisins
    • 2 tbsp cashew / kaju, chopped
    • 2 tbsp maida / plain flour
    • oil, for frying
  • For Onion Tomato Puree
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • 1 onion, sliced
    • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
    • 2 tomato, sliced
    • 2 tbsp cashew
  • For Curry
    • 1 tbsp butter
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • 1 tsp cumin
    • 2 pod cardamom
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 inch cinnamon
    • 2 clove
    • 1 tsp kashmiri red chilli powder
    • ½ tsp turmeric
    • ¾ tsp coriander powder
    • ¼ tsp cumin powder
    • 1 tsp salt
    • ¼ cup cream / malai
    • ½ cup water
    • 1 tsp kasuri methi, crushed
    • ¼ tsp garam masala

Steps

  1. Kofta Preparation
    1. Firstly, in a large mixing bowl take 3 potato and ¾ cup paneer
    2. Add 1 chilli, 2 tbsp coriander, ¼ tsp cumin powder and ½ tsp salt
    3. Add 2 tbsp raisins and 2 tbsp cashew to have a crunchy bite in kofta
    4. Mix well making sure all the spices are well combined
    5. Add 2 tbsp maida and mix well forming a soft dough. maida helps to absorb moisture and bind the mixture well
    6. Prepare a small ball sized kofta by greasing hand with oil
    7. Deep fry on medium hot oil
    8. Stir occasionally, making sure the koftas are cooked uniformly
    9. Fry until the kofta turn golden brown and crisp
    10. Drain off the koftas and keep aside
  2. Curry Preparation for Malai Kofta
    1. Firstly, in a pan heat 2 tbsp oil and saute 1 onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste
    2. Saute until onions changes colour slightly
    3. Further add 2 tomato and saute slightly
    4. Now add 2 tbsp cashew and continue to saute until tomatoes soften completely
    5. Cool completely and transfer to a blender
    6. Blend to smooth paste adding water if required
    7. Now filter the mixture to get rid of skin and seeds
    8. Filter until silky smooth onion-tomato puree is attained. Keep aside
    9. In a large kadai heat 1 tbsp butter and 2 tbsp oil
    10. Saute 1 tsp cumin, 2 pod cardamom, 1 bay leaf, 1 inch cinnamon, 2 clove until it turns aromatic
    11. Further keeping the flame on low, add 1 tsp chilli powder, ½ tsp turmeric,¾ tsp coriander powder and ¼ tsp cumin powder
    12. Saute until the spices turn aromatic
    13. Further add in the prepared onion tomato puree, 1 tsp salt and mix well
    14. Cover and cook until the mixture starts to thicken and oil separates from sides
    15. Now add ¼ cup cream and mix on low flame until it's well combined
    16. Further, add ½ cup water and mix well adjusting consistency as required
    17. Get the curry to a boil, add 1 tsp kasuri methi and ¼ tsp garam masala. mix well
    18. Finally, pour the curry over kofta and malai kofta is ready to enjoy.
